# Hanssem Design Study

A UX and visual design study exploring the digitization, analysis, and modern reinterpretation of a legacy Hanssem interior catalog through editorial systems, spatial organization, and interface thinking.

---

## Overview

This project began as an archival scanning effort and evolved into a broader exploration of visual hierarchy, modular interior systems, and digital UX translation.

Rather than treating the catalog as a static print artifact, the study examines how legacy editorial layouts and product storytelling techniques can inform contemporary digital experiences.

---

## Focus Areas

- Editorial layout systems
- Interior and spatial organization
- UX-oriented visual hierarchy
- Digital preservation
- Responsive presentation design
- Product storytelling

---

## Process

1. Catalog scanning and digitization
2. Image cleanup and alignment correction
3. Spread selection and visual curation
4. UX and design systems analysis
5. HTML/CSS case study presentation

---

## Selected Themes

- Modular kitchen systems
- Material contrast and hierarchy
- Storage and organizational logic
- Brand consistency through color systems
- Print-to-digital design translation

---

## Technologies

- HTML5
- CSS3
- Responsive Design Principles
